PS2

When I was 3, my uncle sent me a gift and it was the PS2 and a bunch of games, among those was Marvel’s Ultimate Alliance. Being a kid who loved the Spiderman and X-Men movies at the time, this would become one of my favorites.

I used to re-watch Spider-Man 1 and 2, The Fantastic Four and the X-Men over and over, I woke up at 5 in the morning to watch old cartoons like He-Man, and always caught the Saturday morning animated cartoons. I’d go to the library and pick up any book in the kid’s section with superheroes on it mostly to look at the pictures and picking up some words here and there because I could only read some at the time. This game would quickly become one of my favorites out of the ones I had. With a high-stakes setup with many Marvel heroes’ arch-nemeses teaming up to create an evil super-army and take over the world, the story brings in heroes from many comics under the Marvel brand with a common cause. The gameplay can be described as a dungeon crawler like Diablo, but I had no idea what that meant at the time of course, all I needed to know was that I could use all the awesome powers of the heroes. The game was faithful to the comics at the time down to their movements and abilities, Spiderman could swing, Storm could fly, Deadpool could teleport, some heroes were able to pick up heavier things than others. The game helped me develop my reading skills and memory in challenges such as Vision’s quiz, strategy by having enemies and bosses only vulnerable to certain elements and combinations, and introduced me to some new superheroes I didn’t know before.
